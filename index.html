As a researcher and PhD student at the Visual Recognition Group (VRG) at FEE, Czech Technical University in Prague, I work under the guidance of prof. Ing. Jiří Matas Ph.D.. My passion lies in advancing machine learning applications for visual recognition tasks, with a particular focus on developing robust computer vision algorithms that perform reliably in real-world scenarios. I'm especially drawn to tackling the challenging last 10% - the details that transform promising research into practical solutions.
My doctoral research centers on analyzing human body dynamics in images and videos. While my primary expertise is in Human Pose Estimation, I work across the full spectrum of human analysis, including Person Detection, Instance Segmentation, 3D Pose and Shape Estimation, and Tracking. To explore my latest research contributions, please visit the Projects and Papers tabs.

Beyond academia, I serve as a coach for Sokol Královské Vinohrady, a floorball team competing in the Czech premier league. When not immersed in research or sports, you'll find me exploring the worlds of psychology and epic fantasy, or in the mountains.
